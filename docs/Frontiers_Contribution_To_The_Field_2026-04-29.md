# Contribution to the Field

This manuscript proposes a testable arithmetic hypothesis for the low-energy
fine-structure constant, using invariants associated with the Bost-Connes system
over the real quadratic field Q(sqrt(5)). The work addresses a foundational
question: although the Standard Model describes the running of the electromagnetic
coupling, the absolute Thomson-limit value of alpha remains an experimental
input. The paper separates exact arithmetic facts from physical hypotheses. The
rigorous layer includes the fundamental unit phi, the regulator log(phi), the
trace Tr(phi^2)=3, and the Dedekind zeta value zeta_K(-3)=1/60, which gives
3!/zeta_K(-3)=360. These data motivate a three-term ansatz for alpha(0)^(-1)
that agrees with CODATA 2022 within 0.58 sigma and with the Fan-Gabrielse 2023
electron magnetic moment determination within 0.08 sigma, while not resolving
the current recoil-input tension. The manuscript is explicit about its
limitations: it does not claim a Standard Model derivation or a completed
Bost-Connes-to-QED construction. Instead, it presents a falsifiable arithmetic
ansatz with a public negative-control screen, a finite post-hypothesis grammar
audit, and concrete failure modes. Its main contribution is therefore a bounded,
reproducible, and testable bridge between arithmetic quantum statistical
mechanics, mathematical physics, and precision QED.
